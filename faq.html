01 / FAQ
Questions

Good to know.

Football, futsal & mini-soccer in Ubud — answered.

Where can I play football in Ubud?

Ubud Jungle Ballers runs open football sessions in central Ubud almost every day of the week — weekday trainings plus social games. Pick a session, bring boots, and show up; new players are always welcome.

Do you play futsal or mini-soccer too?

Yes. Our Thursday "Fun First" session is a mixed-level, mixed-pace community game with futsal and mini-soccer, alongside full-pitch football across the rest of the week.

Can foreigners and expats join, or is it just for locals?

Both. The club is built by Balinese locals and internationals living in or visiting Bali. All nationalities are welcome — locals, expats and travellers alike.

Can women and girls join too?

Yes — Ubud Jungle Ballers is open to everyone. Women are part of our community and welcome at the open sessions. Come down, bring boots, and join in.

I'm not very experienced — can I still play?

Absolutely. We run everything from casual, just-for-fun games to a competitive first team. Beginners and returning players are welcome at the open community sessions.

Do you play in the Gianyar and wider Bali area?

Yes. We're based in Ubud, in the Gianyar regency, and play regional fixtures around Bali — league and cup matches announced each week.

How much does it cost to join?

Joining the community is free. Sign up through the join form or message us on WhatsApp, and you'll be added to the right group within 24 hours.

When are training sessions?

Sessions are around 7 PM. First Team trains Monday and Friday, Second Team on Wednesday, the open "Fun First" community game is Thursday, and regional fixtures are on weekends.
